Sometimes it's not easy to give a simple explanation about oneself, so let's try to make it short: Engineer, Economist, Scientist, Consultant, Psychology Aficionado, Philosopher, Geek, Artist, Photographer, Curious and always ready for new challange.

My friends call me homopedia ("Homo sapiens" [modern human] and "Encyclopedia").

I spent the first 20 years of my life (15 to be precise, I remember that I start reading stuffs at 5) trying to become a Philomath (a seeker of knowledge). However now I realize that the true destination is to become a Polymath (a seeker of wisdom).
The idea developed in Renaissance Italy from the notion expressed by one of its most accomplished representatives, Leon Battista Alberti (1404-1472): that "a man can do all things if he will." It embodied the basic tenets of Renaissance humanism, which considered humans empowered, limitless in their capacities for development, and led to the notion that people should embrace all knowledge and develop their capacities as fully as possible.
